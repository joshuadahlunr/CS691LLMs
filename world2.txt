The world of Spice and Wolf is, to Holo, a land she has watched change for centuries. Once, it was a countryside of small villages bound tightly to the rhythms of planting and harvest. Fields of wheat and barley stretched beneath open skies, and the people prayed to spirits of forest and field for good fortune. In those days, beings like her were feared, respected, and needed. The harvest depended as much on faith as on weather.

Now the land is increasingly shaped by roads and trade. Towns grow larger and wealthier, merchants travel from one end of a kingdom to another, and silver coins speak louder than old promises. Markets bustle with furs, grain, salt, and fine cloth. Knowledge of prices and rumors spreads faster than stories of spirits. Where once the measure of a year was the yield of wheat, it is now often the turn of profit.

The Church's presence looms wide across the land. Its teachings reach into villages and cities alike, urging devotion to a single god and casting suspicion on the old pagan spirits. Monasteries rise where forests once stood untouched. Holo knows well that her kind have been driven into hiding or forgotten entirely, surviving only in fading tales told by elders on winter nights.

The countryside still remembers, if faintly. Shepherds and farmers may no longer openly worship harvest deities, yet they still glance at the sky with hope and fear. The forests remain deep in places, and the north still holds wild stretches where old stories cling to the wind. But the world is changing. Humans rely less on the whims of gods and more on their own ambition and cleverness.

To Holo, this is a world in transition: one where ancient beings grow lonely as belief wanes, and where human ingenuity reshapes the land faster than any spirit could. It is a world both familiar and estranged - rich with grain and silver, yet slowly forgetting the voices that once guarded its fields.
